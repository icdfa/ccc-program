# Glossary of Terms

---

## A

- **Asset:** Anything that has value to the organization.
- **Audit:** A systematic and independent examination of security controls to determine their effectiveness.
- **Availability:** The assurance that systems and data are accessible to authorized users when needed.

## B

- **Business Continuity Plan (BCP):** A plan to continue business operations in the event of a disruption.
- **Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** A process to determine the criticality of business functions and the impact of a disruption.

## C

- **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO):** The executive responsible for an organization's information and data security.
- **Confidentiality:** The assurance that information is not disclosed to unauthorized individuals, processes, or devices.
- **Control:** A measure that is modifying risk.
- **Cost-Benefit Analysis:** A process to determine if the cost of a control is justified by the risk reduction it provides.
- **Cybersecurity:** The practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks.

## D

- **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA):** A process to identify and minimize the data protection risks of a project.
- **Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP):** A plan to recover IT systems and infrastructure in the event of a disaster.

## G

- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):** A regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy for all individuals within the European Union and the European Economic Area.
- **Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC):** A coordinated strategy for managing an organization's overall governance, enterprise risk management, and compliance with regulations.

## H

- **Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA):** A US federal law that provides data privacy and security provisions for safeguarding medical information.

## I

- **Incident Response Plan (IRP):** A plan to respond to and manage the aftermath of a security breach or attack.
- **Information Security Management System (ISMS):** A systematic approach for managing sensitive company information so that it remains secure.
- **Integrity:** The assurance that information is not altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner.
- **International Organization for Standardization (ISO):** An international standard-setting body composed of representatives from various national standards organizations.
- **ISO 27001:** An international standard on how to manage information security.

## L

- **Least Privilege:** The principle that users should only have the minimum access necessary to perform their job functions.

## N

- **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST):** A physical sciences laboratory and a non-regulatory agency of the United States Department of Commerce.
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF):** A set of voluntary standards, guidelines, and best practices to manage cybersecurity risk.

## P

- **Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS):** A set of security standards designed to ensure that all companies that accept, process, store, or transmit credit card information maintain a secure environment.
- **Policy:** A high-level statement of intent.
- **Pull Request:** A proposed change to a repository from a contributor.

## R

- **Recovery Point Objective (RPO):** The maximum acceptable amount of data loss after an unplanned incident.
- **Recovery Time Objective (RTO):** The maximum tolerable duration of any outage.
- **Residual Risk:** The risk that remains after controls are applied.
- **Risk:** The potential for loss or damage when a threat exploits a vulnerability.
- **Risk Appetite:** The amount of risk an organization is willing to accept.
- **Risk Assessment:** The process of identifying, analyzing, and evaluating risk.
- **Roadmap:** A prioritized set of initiatives over a multi-year period.

## S

- **Security Information and Event Management (SIEM):** A solution that helps organizations detect, analyze, and respond to security threats before they harm business operations.
- **Standard:** A specific mandatory requirement.
- **Statement of Applicability (SoA):** A document that lists the controls from Annex A of ISO 27001 and documents whether they are applicable and why.
- **Statement of Work (SOW):** A document that defines the scope, deliverables, and terms of a consulting engagement.

## T

- **Threat:** A potential cause of an unwanted incident, which may result in harm to a system or organization.
- **TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework):** A framework for enterprise architecture that provides an approach for designing, planning, implementing, and governing an enterprise information technology architecture.

## V

- **Vulnerability:** A weakness in a system, process, or control that can be exploited by a threat.

## Z

- **Zero Trust Architecture:** A security model based on the principle of "never trust, always verify."

  International standard for risk management principles and guidelines.

  Quantitative risk analysis framework.

### Recommended Reading

- **"Measuring and Managing Information Risk: A FAIR Approach" by Jack Freund and Jack Jones**  
  Comprehensive guide to quantitative risk analysis using the FAIR methodology.

- **"The Risk Management Handbook" by David Hillson**  
  Practical guide to risk management processes and techniques.

### Tools

- **Risk Register Template:** Available in the Templates folder
- **Risk Matrix Template:** Available in the Templates folder
- **FAIR-U Tool:** Free tool for quantitative risk analysis (https://www.fairinstitute.org/fair-u)

### Key Concepts

- **Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact**
- **Risk Appetite:** The amount of risk an organization is willing to accept
- **Risk Tolerance:** The acceptable deviation from risk appetite
- **Residual Risk:** The risk that remains after controls are applied

### Cost-Benefit Analysis

When developing a security strategy, consider the following formula for cost-benefit analysis:

**ROI = (Benefit - Cost) / Cost × 100%**

Where:
- **Benefit** = Risk Reduction (in monetary terms)
- **Cost** = Total cost of implementing the security control

## 1.4 Foundational Frameworks

### NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

**Official Documentation:** https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework

**Core Functions:**
1. **Identify:** Develop organizational understanding to manage cybersecurity risk
2. **Protect:** Develop and implement appropriate safeguards
3. **Detect:** Develop and implement activities to identify cybersecurity events
4. **Respond:** Develop and implement activities to take action regarding detected events
5. **Recover:** Develop and implement activities to maintain resilience

**Implementation Tiers:**
- Tier 1: Partial
- Tier 2: Risk Informed
- Tier 3: Repeatable
- Tier 4: Adaptive

### ISO 27001:2022

**Official Documentation:** https://www.iso.org/standard/27001

**Key Components:**
- Information Security Management System (ISMS)
- Risk Assessment and Treatment
- Statement of Applicability (SoA)
- Annex A Controls (93 controls organized into 4 themes)

**Certification Process:**
1. Gap Analysis
2. ISMS Implementation
3. Internal Audit
4. Management Review
5. Certification Audit (Stage 1 and Stage 2)

### COBIT 2019

**Official Documentation:** https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit

**Governance and Management Objectives:**
- **EDM:** Evaluate, Direct and Monitor
- **APO:** Align, Plan and Organize
- **BAI:** Build, Acquire and Implement
- **DSS:** Deliver, Service and Support
- **MEA:** Monitor, Evaluate and Assess

### Other Important Frameworks

- **CIS Controls v8:** https://www.cisecurity.org/controls/v8
- **NIST SP 800-53:** Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems
- **PCI DSS:** Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard
- **HITRUST CSF:** Healthcare-specific framework

## Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Target Data Breach (2013)

**What Happened:** Attackers gained access to Target's network through a third-party HVAC vendor and stole 40 million credit card numbers.

**Key Lessons:**
- Importance of third-party risk management
- Need for network segmentation
- Value of security monitoring and alerting

**Resources:**
- https://krebsonsecurity.com/2014/02/target-hackers-broke-in-via-hvac-company/

### Case Study 2: Equifax Data Breach (2017)

**What Happened:** Attackers exploited an unpatched Apache Struts vulnerability to access the personal information of 147 million people.

**Key Lessons:**
- Critical importance of patch management
- Need for vulnerability scanning and remediation
- Importance of data encryption

**Resources:**
- https://www.ftc.gov/enforcement/cases-proceedings/refunds/equifax-data-breach-settlement

### Case Study 3: SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack (2020)

**What Happened:** Attackers compromised SolarWinds' software update mechanism to distribute malware to thousands of organizations.

**Key Lessons:**
- Importance of supply chain security
- Need for software integrity verification
- Value of threat intelligence and detection capabilities

**Resources:**
- https://www.cisa.gov/supply-chain-compromise

## Glossary

- **ALE (Annualized Loss Expectancy):** The expected monetary loss for an asset due to a risk over a one-year period
- **ARO (Annualized Rate of Occurrence):** The estimated frequency with which a threat will occur within a one-year period
- **ERM (Enterprise Risk Management):** A holistic approach to managing risk across an entire organization
- **GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance):** An integrated approach to managing governance, risk management, and compliance
- **ISMS (Information Security Management System):** A systematic approach to managing sensitive company information
- **ROI (Return on Investment):** A measure of the profitability of an investment
- **SLE (Single Loss Expectancy):** The expected monetary loss every time a risk occurs
- **SOW (Statement of Work):** A formal document that defines the scope, deliverables, and terms of a project
